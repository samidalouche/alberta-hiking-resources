## 🏕️ What This Event Is All About

Each year, we reserve a [massive group campground](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/bow-valley-pp/information-facilities/group-use/grouse/) in Bow Valley Provincial Park, split the cost, and create our own little weekend hiking village. 🏔️

**Daytime is completely free-form** — choose your own adventures, hikes, lazy mornings, or explorations. ☀️🥾

**Evenings? That's when the magic happens.** We all gather around a roaring communal campfire for stories, laughter, and reconnecting under the stars. 🔥🌌

::tip
**Not camping?** No problem! You're still very welcome to swing by the campfire on Friday and/or Saturday night. 👋
::

## 📅 When

The gathering takes place over **a Friday + Saturday night in early June** — typically the first weekend, when the snow is mostly gone from the valley and the bugs aren't out in force yet. 🌿

Exact dates are confirmed in the fall of the previous year.

::tip
**Don't miss the announcement!** The event is shared on the [Alberta Hikers Together](https://www.facebook.com/groups/albertahikerstogether) Facebook group. Join the group and [adjust your notification settings](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v5ArW00zizA) so it doesn't slip past you. 🔔
::

## 📍 Where

[Grouse Group Campground](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/bow-valley-pp/information-facilities/group-use/grouse/) in Bow Valley Provincial Park, Kananaskis — about an hour west of Calgary, right at the doorstep of the Rockies. 🏔️

It's a dedicated group-use site, which means we have the whole place to ourselves: room for tents, trailers, RVs, vans, and one big shared firepit. 🔥

## 💰 Cost

We split the campground fees published on the [Grouse Group Campground page](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/bow-valley-pp/information-facilities/group-use/grouse/) — per sleeping unit, not per person.

## 🐦 Early Birds vs. 🦋 Butterflies

Every gathering has its rhythm — and ours has two.

The **Early Birds** arrive first. In the quiet of fall, while the rest of the world is still folding up summer, they commit. They send word, they send funds, they hold the campground for the rest of us. They are the steady ones — the keepers of the nest, the reason there is a gathering at all. 🐦

And then there are the **butterflies**. 🦋

Butterflies don't plan in seasons; they plan in moments. They drift in when the wind feels right — sometimes a month before, sometimes the morning of. They might stay both nights, or just one, or only long enough to share a drink by the fire and slip back into the dark. They are weather-watchers, mood-followers, last-minute yes-sayers. They follow the warmth, the colours, the pull of the flame.

Both belong. The Early Birds build the gathering. The butterflies bring it to life. 💛

## 🎒 What to Bring

- 🛏️ Your sleeping setup — tent, sleeping bag, mattress, or whatever keeps you cozy
- 🍳 Your own food + drinks
- 💧 Drinking water — there's none on site
- 🪵 A camp chair for fireside hangs
- 🥾 Hiking gear for whatever day-adventures you're planning
- 🌧️ Layers + rain gear + sun protection — Alberta weather can swing wildly in early June
- 🎵 Optional: an instrument, a bottle of something nice to share, a story to tell

## 📬 Interested?

Send a message to [Sami](/hike-organizers/sami). You'll first be added to the **butterfly chat**, where reminders go out as the event gets closer — no commitment required.

Once you confirm, you'll be moved to the **main chat** for that year's edition. That's where all the day-to-day logistics happen: who's bringing what, hike plans, carpools, weather updates. 💬

::warning
**Don't rely on Facebook event notifications.** They're notoriously unreliable — delayed, missing, or silently dropped. The only way to be sure you won't miss anything is to message Sami directly and get added to the chat. 📵
::

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### 🔗 Where can I find the latest FB event?

- **June 2026 edition** → [facebook.com/share/1Dp4qLQFAr](https://www.facebook.com/share/1Dp4qLQFAr/)

### 📣 Where are the events posted?

On the [Alberta Hikers Together](https://www.facebook.com/groups/albertahikerstogether) Facebook group — specifically in the [Events](https://www.facebook.com/groups/albertahikerstogether/events) tab. 📅

To make sure you don't miss it, **enable all notifications for the group**. Here's a [short video walkthrough](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v5ArW00zizA) on how to do that. 🔔

### 🥾 Is there a group hike?

Everyone plans their days the way they want — daytime is completely unstructured. That said, we usually do **one group hike on the Saturday** for those who want something on the easier side: more social, lower-stakes, and back at camp early enough to enjoy the fire. 🔥

Absolutely no pressure to join though — the official event is really about the time around the fire. 💛

### 🦋 What is a butterfly?

See the [Early Birds vs. Butterflies](#early-birds-vs-butterflies) section above. 💛

### 👶 Can I bring my kids?

Yes — kids are very welcome. Every year there are usually a few other families who bring theirs too, so they'll likely find some company around the fire. 🔥

### 🧓 Am I too old for this?

Not at all. Every year we welcome people from all ages — the gathering is about the fire and the connection, not the kilometres. 💛

### 🚿 Are there showers?

No showers at the campground — only dry toilets. If you'd like a shower, you can head to [Bow Valley Campground](https://maps.app.goo.gl/tMVc8AzkSKkcguTU7) located 15 minutes away, which has token showers. 🚿

### 💧 Is there drinking water?

No drinking water on site, so bring your own. 💧 The campground does provide a large container of water for dishwashing and similar tasks. 🍽️
