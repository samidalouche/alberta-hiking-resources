## 🏕️ What This Event Is All About

Each year, we reserve a [massive group campground](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/bow-valley-pp/information-facilities/group-use/grouse/) in Bow Valley Provincial Park, split the cost, and create our own little weekend hiking village. 🏔️

**Daytime is completely free-form** — choose your own adventures, hikes, lazy mornings, or explorations. ☀️🥾

**Evenings? That's when the magic happens.** We all gather around a roaring communal campfire for stories, laughter, and reconnecting under the stars. 🔥🌌

::tip
**Not camping?** No problem! You're still very welcome to swing by the campfire on Friday and/or Saturday night. 👋
::


## ✨ The Vibe We're Going For

This isn't a corporate retreat with a packed agenda and a facilitator. It's **a group of friends, an organic and unstructured weekend** — and **you are the vibe**. 💛 There's no program to follow and no one performing; the gathering is whatever the people who show up make of it.

A few things we do on purpose to protect that spirit:

- **Friday's potluck is the icebreaker.** 🍲 Sharing food is the easiest way in, so newcomers get a relaxed chance to meet the tribe before the weekend really gets going.
- **Everything is optional.** The Saturday social hike and the potluck are invitations, not obligations — no pressure, and no competition with whatever else you'd rather do. We're just offering options; vibe the way you want. 🌿
- **Small and beautiful, not one big monolith.** Rather than herding everyone into a single circle, we set things up so smaller clusters form naturally — tables pulled close to the fire, a second firepit going, room to drift between them. 🔥 If you're on the shyer side, you should never feel like you're giving a speech to a crowd.
- **Friday tends to be the bigger night.** It's organic and we don't engineer it, but the energy usually peaks around the Friday potluck and fire. By Saturday things are slower — folks are tired from the day's adventures and tend to turn in earlier. 🌙

The gathering takes place over **a Friday + Saturday night in early June** — typically the first weekend, when the snow is mostly gone from the valley and the bugs aren't out in force yet. 🌿

## 📍 Where

[Grouse Group Campground](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/bow-valley-pp/information-facilities/group-use/grouse/) in Bow Valley Provincial Park, Kananaskis — about an hour west of Calgary, right at the doorstep of the Rockies. 🏔️

It's a dedicated group-use site, which means we have the whole place to ourselves: room for tents, trailers, RVs, vans, and one big shared firepit. 🔥

## 💰 Cost

For this edition's actual price, check [this year's event](#where-can-i-find-the-latest-fb-event). 👇

We split the campground fees published on the [Grouse Group Campground page](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/bow-valley-pp/information-facilities/group-use/grouse/) — per sleeping unit, not per person.

Alberta Parks prices the site unevenly: the first sleeping units cost more than each additional one. Rather than let that fall on whoever happens to book first, **we pool the total and re-shuffle it ourselves** so the cost comes out fair between early birds and butterflies alike. 🐦🦋 It's an approximate split — we don't know exactly who's coming ahead of time — but it evens things out as best we can.

A few details:

- Charged **per sleeping unit** — tent, trailer, RV, or van — not per person.
- **Sleeping in your car?** That counts too, with a small charge to help cover the fees. 🚗
- Any **leftover funds carry over** to the next year's gathering, easing everyone's share down the road. 💛

::warning
A [Kananaskis Conservation Pass](https://www.alberta.ca/kananaskis-conservation-pass.aspx) is required for every vehicle in Kananaskis Country — make sure you've got one before you arrive. 🎫
::

## 🐦 Early Birds vs. 🦋 Butterflies

Every gathering has its rhythm — and ours has two.

The **Early Birds** arrive first. In the quiet of fall, while the rest of the world is still folding up summer, they commit. They send word, they send funds, they hold the campground for the rest of us. They are the steady ones — the keepers of the nest, the reason there is a gathering at all. 🐦

And then there are the **butterflies**. 🦋

Butterflies don't plan in seasons; they plan in moments. They drift in when the wind feels right — sometimes a month before, sometimes the morning of. They might stay both nights, or just one, or only long enough to share a drink by the fire and slip back into the dark. Maybe a shift got added, maybe the schedule shuffled at the last minute — life moves, and so do they. They are weather-watchers, mood-followers, last-minute yes-sayers. They follow the warmth, the colours, the pull of the flame.

Both belong. The Early Birds build the gathering. The butterflies bring it to life. 💛

### 📣 Where are the events posted?

### 🥾 Is there a group hike?

Everyone plans their days the way they want — daytime is completely unstructured. That said, we usually do **one group hike on the Saturday** — more social, lower-stakes, and back at camp early enough to enjoy the fire. 🔥 We aim for something shorter, but heads up: summit hikes in this area almost always involve real elevation, so even the shorter options are usually not suitable for beginners.

Absolutely no pressure to join though — the official event is really about the time around the fire. 💛

### 🦋 What is a butterfly?

See the [Early Birds vs. Butterflies](#early-birds-vs-butterflies) section above. 💛

### 👶 Can I bring my kids?

Yes — kids are very welcome. Every year there are usually a few other families who bring theirs too, so they'll likely find some company around the fire. 🔥

### 🧓 Am I too old for this?

Not at all. Every year we welcome people from all ages — the gathering is about the fire and the connection, not the kilometres. 💛

### 🌦️ What will the weather be like?

Early June in the valley is unpredictable — warm sun, cold nights, rain, and the odd surprise can all land in one weekend. ☀️🌧️❄️ Pack layers and plan for cold nights no matter what the forecast says.

For the closest read on conditions, check the **[Exshaw and Seebe forecasts](/weather-trail-conditions/popular-locations#canmore-and-north-kananaskis)** on our weather page — they're the two towns nearest the campground, and the cards there cover both the weather and the smoke / air-quality forecast. 🔥💨

### 🏕️ I've never camped — what do I need?

The essentials:

- **Tent** ⛺
- **Sleeping bag** 🛏️
- **Sleeping mattress** (a pad between you and the ground)

Recommended:

- **A camping chair** for around the fire 🪑

Nights in June can get **very cold**, so don't skimp on the sleeping bag and mattress:

- **Sleeping bag:** add about 10°C to whatever temperature it's advertised for — the rating is optimistic, to put it kindly. 😅
- **Mattress:** just as important as the sleeping bag. It insulates you from the cold ground, which is where a lot of the chill comes from. 🥶

Need to pick up gear? [Decathlon Calgary](https://www.decathlon.ca) has solid, fairly priced options. 🛒 For more, see the [Outdoor Gear](/outdoor-gear) section. 🎒

### 🚿 Are there showers?

No showers at the campground — only dry toilets. If you'd like a shower, you can head to [Bow Valley Campground](https://maps.app.goo.gl/tMVc8AzkSKkcguTU7) located 15 minutes away, which has token showers. 🚿

### 💧 Is there drinking water?

No drinking water on site, so bring your own. 💧 The campground does provide a large container of water for dishwashing and similar tasks. 🍽️

### 📍 Where exactly is the campground?

[Grouse Group Campground](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/bow-valley-pp/information-facilities/group-use/grouse/) in Bow Valley Provincial Park, Kananaskis. 🏔️ Drop the [Google Maps pin](https://maps.app.goo.gl/uL4Wk5aYzWA7hzDR6) into your navigation to find your way there. 🚗

### 📶 Is there cell reception at the campground?

Yes — but don't count on it for Netflix. 📵 It's a bit spotty, though good enough for synchronizing through text/Messenger, and sending a few pictures if you're lucky. 📸

### 😴 How noisy is the campground? I'm a light sleeper.

The campground is huge, so there's plenty of room to spread out. 🏕️ If you're a light sleeper, head to the **very far end of the campground** — it's quiet and peaceful out there, well away from the fire. 🌙

### 👋 Can I swing by if I'm not camping?

Absolutely! You're very welcome to come say hi and join us around the fire — Friday and/or Saturday night. See the [Join the Fire & Potluck](#join-the-fire-potluck) section above for details. Just let me know you're coming. 🔥
