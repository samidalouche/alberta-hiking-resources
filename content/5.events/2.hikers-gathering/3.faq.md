## 🥾 Is there a group hike?

Everyone plans their days the way they want — daytime is completely unstructured. That said, we usually do **one group hike on the Saturday** — more social, lower-stakes, and back at camp early enough to enjoy the fire. 🔥 We aim for something shorter, but heads up: summit hikes in this area almost always involve real elevation, so even the shorter options are usually not suitable for beginners.

Absolutely no pressure to join though — the official event is really about the time around the fire. 💛

## 👶 Can I bring my kids?

Yes — kids are very welcome. Every year there are usually a few other families who bring theirs too, so they'll likely find some company around the fire. 🔥

## 🧓 Am I too old for this?

Not at all. Every year we welcome people from all ages — the gathering is about the fire and the connection, not the kilometres. 💛

## 🌦️ What will the weather be like?

Early June in the valley is unpredictable — warm sun, cold nights, rain, and the odd surprise can all land in one weekend. ☀️🌧️❄️ Pack layers and plan for cold nights no matter what the forecast says.

For the closest read on conditions, check the **[Exshaw and Seebe forecasts](/weather-trail-conditions/popular-locations#canmore-and-north-kananaskis)** on our weather page — they're the two towns nearest the campground, and the cards there cover both the weather and the smoke / air-quality forecast. 🔥💨

## 🏕️ I've never camped — what do I need?

The essentials:

- **Tent** ⛺
- **Sleeping bag** 🛏️
- **Sleeping mattress** (a pad between you and the ground)

Recommended:

- **A camping chair** for around the fire 🪑

Nights in June can get **very cold**, so don't skimp on the sleeping bag and mattress:

- **Sleeping bag:** add about 10°C to whatever temperature it's advertised for — the rating is optimistic, to put it kindly. 😅
- **Mattress:** just as important as the sleeping bag. It insulates you from the cold ground, which is where a lot of the chill comes from. 🥶

Need to pick up gear? [Decathlon Calgary](https://www.decathlon.ca) has solid, fairly priced options. 🛒 For more, see the [Outdoor Gear](/outdoor-gear) section. 🎒

## 🚿 Are there showers?

No showers at the campground — only dry toilets. If you'd like a shower, you can head to [Bow Valley Campground](https://maps.app.goo.gl/tMVc8AzkSKkcguTU7) located 15 minutes away, which has token showers. 🚿

## 💧 Is there drinking water?

No drinking water on site, so bring your own. 💧 The campground does provide a large container of water for dishwashing and similar tasks. 🍽️

## 📍 Where exactly is the campground?

[Grouse Group Campground](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/bow-valley-pp/information-facilities/group-use/grouse/) in Bow Valley Provincial Park, Kananaskis. 🏔️ Drop the [Google Maps pin](https://maps.app.goo.gl/uL4Wk5aYzWA7hzDR6) into your navigation to find your way there. 🚗

## 📶 Is there cell reception at the campground?

Yes — but don't count on it for Netflix. 📵 It's a bit spotty, though good enough for synchronizing through text/Messenger, and sending a few pictures if you're lucky. 📸

## 😴 How noisy is the campground? I'm a light sleeper.

The campground is huge, so there's plenty of room to spread out. 🏕️ If you're a light sleeper, head to the **very far end of the campground** — it's quiet and peaceful out there, well away from the fire. 🌙

## 👋 Can I swing by if I'm not camping?

Absolutely! You're very welcome to come say hi and join us around the fire — Friday and/or Saturday night. See the [Join the Fire & Potluck](/events/hikers-gathering/plan-your-weekend#join-the-fire-potluck) section for details. Just let me know you're coming. 🔥
