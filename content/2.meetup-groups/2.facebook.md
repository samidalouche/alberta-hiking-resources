## Recommended Groups

These Facebook groups are useful for finding hiking, scrambling, and even climbing partners. They're grassroots communities—there's no central organizer or hierarchy, just like-minded hikers sharing adventures, suggesting outings, and connecting over a shared love of the outdoors. If you're after a flexible, community-driven way to meet hiking partners, they're a great place to start.

::tip
**Not sure where to start?** Two of these groups exist specifically to find partners (**Alberta Hikers Together** and **Find Hiking Groups/Partners, Alberta**), while **Alberta Mountain-aholics** is a tighter-knit community where partnerships form as a byproduct of connecting with people first.
::

## How they compare

| Group | Privacy | Focus | Best for |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Alberta Hikers Together](/meetup-groups/alberta-hikers-together) | Public | Posting **specific** hikes (date, time, trail) | Organizing a concrete outing others can join |
| [Find Hiking Groups/Partners, Alberta](/meetup-groups/find-hiking-groups-partners-alberta) | Private | Finding partners—specific hikes or open-ended | Casting a wide net; the biggest, most active group |
| [Alberta Mountain-aholics](/meetup-groups/alberta-mountain-aholics) | Private | Community first: trail conditions, courses, routes | Intermediate/advanced hikers who want to build connections |

A few notes to go with the table:

- **Alberta Hikers Together** and **Find Hiking Groups/Partners, Alberta** have very similar goals and rules. The main difference: Hikers Together encourages posting a specific hike with a meeting time, whereas Find Hiking Groups/Partners has more open-ended "looking for buddies" posts.
- Because **Alberta Hikers Together** is public, it's the easiest place to create an event or post first, then cross-share to the other (private) groups.
- **Alberta Mountain-aholics** isn't primarily about finding partners. People post about trail conditions, avalanche and crevasse rescue courses, scrambling and climbing routes, indoor climbing, and mountaineering objectives—partnerships tend to grow out of those interactions.
