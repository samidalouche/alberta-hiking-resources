Want to sleep close to the trailhead? This page covers the three main ways to do it — **campgrounds**, **hostels**, and **backcountry huts** — plus how the booking systems work and which spots are reservable versus first-come, first-served.

## 🗓️ How Booking Works

- **Parks Canada (National Parks)** publishes the [reservation launch dates](https://parks.canada.ca/voyage-travel/reserve) in November or December for the following summer. Reservations launch on different dates for each park, usually spread between mid-January and mid-February.
  - [Reservation Website](https://reservation.pc.gc.ca/)
- **Alberta Parks (Provincial Parks)** uses a [rolling-window system](https://albertaparks.ca/albertaparksca/visit-our-parks/camping-in-alberta-parks/online-reservations/): reservations open 90 days in advance for individual and backcountry camping, and 180 days in advance for group and comfort camping.
  - [Reservation Website](https://albertaparks.ca/reserve/)
- **BC Parks** takes reservations through [camping.bcparks.ca](https://camping.bcparks.ca/) on a rolling window — check the site for the current booking window.
- **US National Park Service (NPS)** — in particular [Montana's Glacier National Park](https://www.nps.gov/glac/planyourvisit/reservation-campgrounds.htm) just south of the border — uses a rolling window with reservations up to 6 months in advance.
  - [Reservation Website](https://www.recreation.gov/)

**🔔 Sold out? Get alerted about cancellations.** These services watch the reservation systems and notify you when a spot frees up — you still book it yourself through the official site:
- [Schnerp](https://www.schnerp.com/): Monitors campground reservation sites for cancellations. Comes with 10 free alerts, with paid plans for more.
- [Scree Alerts](https://screealerts.com/): Watches high-demand backcountry campsites across Parks Canada and BC Parks (currently in beta).

## 🏕️ Frontcountry Campgrounds & Hostels by Area

**These are all frontcountry campgrounds** — car-accessible, drive-up sites you use as a basecamp, not backcountry sites you hike into. (For huts you hike to, see [Backcountry Huts](#backcountry-huts) below.)

The areas below mirror the [Popular Locations](/weather-trail-conditions/popular-locations) weather page, so you can line up the forecast with a place to sleep. For each area, the important campgrounds are grouped by how you get a spot:

- **Reservable** — book ahead through the operator's reservation system (see above).
- **First-come, first-served (FCFS)** — no reservations; you claim a site in person, so arrive early on summer weekends.

::warning
**Always confirm before you go.** Reservable-vs-FCFS status, operating seasons, and closures change from year to year, and wildfires and floods can shut sites down at short notice. Treat this as a starting point and check the official link for current details.
::

### Bragg Creek & Elbow Valley

*There's no campground at West Bragg Creek itself — the nearest basecamps sit along Highway 66 (Elbow Valley) and at McLean Creek. (Highway 66 is gated in winter.)*

- **Reservable** — [McLean Creek](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/mclean-creek-pra/information-facilities/camping/mclean-creek/) (year-round), [Little Elbow](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/little-elbow-pra/information-facilities/camping/little-elbow/), [Paddy's Flat](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/elbow-river-pra/information-facilities/camping/paddy-s-flat/) *(also has FCFS sites)*
- **First-come, first-served** — [Beaver Flats](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/elbow-falls-pra/information-facilities/camping/beaver-flats/)

### Canmore and North Kananaskis

All in **Bow Valley Provincial Park** (Alberta Parks), near Exshaw/Seebe. There's no frontcountry campground at Barrier Lake itself — it's a day-use trailhead.

- **Reservable** — [Bow Valley](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/bow-valley-pp/information-facilities/camping/bow-valley/), [Willow Rock](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/bow-valley-pp/information-facilities/camping/willow-rock/), and [others](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/bow-valley-pp/information-facilities/)
- **First-come, first-served** — [Three Sisters](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/bow-valley-pp/information-facilities/camping/three-sisters/)
- **Hostels** — [HI Kananaskis Wilderness Hostel](https://hihostels.ca/en/destinations/hi-kananaskis) (Ribbon Creek, down Highway 40 — the main hostel basecamp for both North and South Kananaskis)

### South Kananaskis

Spread across **Peter Lougheed**, **Spray Valley**, and **Sheep River** provincial parks (Alberta Parks).

- **Reservable** — [Elkwood](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/peter-lougheed-pp/information-facilities/camping/elkwood/), [Lower Lake](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/peter-lougheed-pp/information-facilities/camping/lower-lake/), [Interlakes](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/peter-lougheed-pp/information-facilities/camping/interlakes/), and [Boulton Creek](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/peter-lougheed-pp/information-facilities/) (Peter Lougheed PP); [Sandy McNabb](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/sheep-river-pp/information-facilities/camping/sandy-mcnabb/) (Sheep River PP)
- **First-come, first-served** — [Canyon](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/peter-lougheed-pp/information-facilities/camping/canyon/) and [Mount Sarrail](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/peter-lougheed-pp/information-facilities/) (walk-in tents, Peter Lougheed PP); [Eau Claire](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/spray-valley-pp/information-facilities/camping/eau-claire/) and [Spray Lakes West](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/spray-valley-pp/information-facilities/camping/spray-lakes-west/) (Spray Valley PP); [Bluerock](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/sheep-river-pp/information-facilities/camping/bluerock/) (Sheep River PP)
- **Hostels** — [HI Kananaskis Wilderness Hostel](https://hihostels.ca/en/destinations/hi-kananaskis) (Ribbon Creek, Highway 40)

### Banff, Lake Louise, and Yoho

**Banff National Park** — [camping & booking](https://parks.canada.ca/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/camping)

- **Reservable** — Tunnel Mountain (Village I & II and Trailer Court), Two Jack Lakeside, Two Jack Main (Banff townsite); Johnston Canyon, Castle Mountain, Protection Mountain (Bow Valley Parkway); Lake Louise (tent & trailer). All Banff and Lake Louise frontcountry campgrounds are reservable.

**Yoho National Park, BC** — [camping & booking](https://parks.canada.ca/pn-np/bc/yoho/activ/camping/avantpays-frontcountry)

- **Reservable** — Kicking Horse, Takakkaw Falls (walk-in, tents only), Monarch *(FCFS in shoulder season)*
- **First-come, first-served** — Hoodoo Creek

::note
**Lake O'Hara** is a special case: no private vehicles, so you either hike the 11 km access road or ride the reserved Parks Canada shuttle bus. Both the day-use bus and the campground are allocated by advance reservation / random draw earlier in the year — see [Plan a trip to Lake O'Hara](https://parks.canada.ca/pn-np/bc/yoho/activ/randonnee-hike/ohara/visit).
::

- **Hostels** — [HI Banff Alpine Centre](https://hihostels.ca/en/destinations/hi-banff), [HI Lake Louise Alpine Centre](https://hihostels.ca/en/destinations/hi-lake-louise), [HI Castle Mountain Wilderness Hostel](https://hihostels.ca/en/destinations/hi-castle-mountain) (Bow Valley Parkway). Note: [HI Yoho / Whiskey Jack](https://hihostels.ca/en/destinations/hi-yoho-national-park) is temporarily closed — verify before relying on it.

### Abraham Lake, Nordegg, and Saskatchewan River Crossing

**David Thompson Country (Nordegg / Abraham Lake)** — Alberta Parks

- **Reservable** — Fish Lake / Shunda Lake recreation area (book via [Alberta Parks](https://albertaparks.ca/reserve/)); [Crescent Falls](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/central/crescent-falls-pra/information-facilities/camping/crescent-falls/) *(also has FCFS sites)*

**Icefields Parkway — Banff National Park** — [camping & booking](https://parks.canada.ca/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/camping)

- **Reservable** — Rampart Creek, Silverhorn Creek
- **First-come, first-served** — Mosquito Creek, Waterfowl Lakes

**Icefields Parkway & Jasper — Jasper National Park** — [camping & booking](https://parks.canada.ca/pn-np/ab/jasper/activ/passez-stay/camping)

- **Reservable** — Whistlers, Wapiti, Miette (townsite area)
- **First-come, first-served** — Snaring (townsite); Wilcox, Columbia Icefield (tents) and the Icefields Centre RV lot, Jonas Creek, Honeymoon Lake, Mount Kerkeslin (along the Parkway)

::warning
**Jasper 2024 wildfire.** The July 2024 wildfire closed or destroyed a number of Jasper-area facilities. Some campgrounds (e.g. Wabasso) remain closed, and several hostels were lost. Confirm current status on the [Jasper National Park camping page](https://parks.canada.ca/pn-np/ab/jasper/activ/passez-stay/camping) before planning around them.
::

- **Hostels** — [HI Nordegg / Shunda Creek](https://hihostels.ca/en/destinations/hi-nordegg); along the Icefields Parkway: [HI Mosquito Creek](https://hihostels.ca/en/destinations/hi-mosquito-creek), [HI Rampart Creek](https://hihostels.ca/en/destinations/hi-rampart-creek), [HI Hilda Creek](https://hihostels.ca/en/destinations/hi-hilda-creek) (group rental only), and [HI Beauty Creek](https://hihostels.ca/en/destinations/alberta/hi-beauty-creek). Note: HI Jasper, HI Athabasca Falls, and HI Maligne Canyon were lost or closed in the 2024 wildfire.

### Waterton Lakes

**Waterton Lakes National Park** — [camping & booking](https://parks.canada.ca/pn-np/ab/waterton/activ/camping)

- **Reservable** — [Townsite](https://parks.canada.ca/pn-np/ab/waterton/activ/camping/townsite-camping) *(FCFS in shoulder season)*
- **First-come, first-served** — [Belly River](https://parks.canada.ca/pn-np/ab/waterton/activ/camping/camping-belly)
- **Crandell Mountain** was destroyed in the 2017 Kenow wildfire and is being rebuilt — confirm status before counting on it.
- **Hostels** — none; Hostelling International has no property in Waterton.

### Crowsnest Pass

**Crowsnest Pass & Castle region** — Alberta Parks

- **Reservable** — [Chinook Lake](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/south/chinook-pra/information-facilities/camping/chinook/); [Beaver Mines Lake](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/south/castle-pp/information-facilities/camping/beaver-mines-lake/), [Castle Falls](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/south/castle-pp/information-facilities/camping/castle-falls/), and Castle River Bridge (Castle PP)
- **First-come, first-served** — Lynx Creek and the numbered designated camping areas ([Castle PP camping index](https://www.albertaparks.ca/parks/south/castle-pp/information-facilities/camping/))
- **Hostels** — none nearby.

### Other BC National Parks

**Glacier National Park, BC (Rogers Pass)** — [camping & booking](https://parks.canada.ca/pn-np/bc/glacier/activ/passez-stay/camping)

- **Reservable** — Loop Brook; Illecillewaet *(roughly half reservable, half FCFS)*
- **Mount Sir Donald** is closed until further notice.

**Mount Revelstoke National Park** — [camping & booking](https://parks.canada.ca/pn-np/bc/revelstoke/activ/passez-stay/camping)

- **Reservable** — Snowforest (the park's only frontcountry campground; hard-sided units, and walk-in tent sites have been temporarily unavailable — verify)

**Near Revelstoke (BC Parks)** — [Martha Creek](https://bcparks.ca/martha-creek-park/) and [Blanket Creek](https://bcparks.ca/blanket-creek-park/), both a mix of reservable and FCFS sites.

**Golden, BC** — the practical basecamp for Golden is the Yoho National Park campgrounds just east of town (see [Banff, Lake Louise, and Yoho](#banff-lake-louise-and-yoho) above).

- **Hostels** — no Hostelling International property in Rogers Pass, Revelstoke, or Golden; the nearest ([HI Yoho / Whiskey Jack](https://hihostels.ca/en/destinations/hi-yoho-national-park)) is temporarily closed.

### Glacier National Park, Montana (USA)

Just across the border, and a popular cross-border objective for the community. Reservable sites book on [Recreation.gov](https://www.recreation.gov/gateways/2725) on a rolling window (see the NPS [reservation campgrounds](https://www.nps.gov/glac/planyourvisit/reservation-campgrounds.htm) and [first-come, first-served](https://www.nps.gov/glac/planyourvisit/first-come-first-served-campgrounds.htm) pages).

- **Reservable** — Apgar, Fish Creek, Sprague Creek, Avalanche (Going-to-the-Sun Road corridor); Rising Sun, St. Mary (east); Many Glacier
- **First-come, first-served** — Bowman Lake, Kintla Lake, Logging Creek, Quartz Creek (North Fork, unpaved roads), and Cut Bank (east) — all small and quick to fill; arrive early
- **Two Medicine** (normally reservable) is closed for the 2026 season for construction — verify before planning around it.
- **Hostels** — Hostelling International has no property in Montana. (A US **park entrance pass** is required; a separate vehicle/timed-entry reservation was *not* required as of the 2026 season, but confirm on [nps.gov/glac](https://www.nps.gov/glac/planyourvisit/index.htm).)

## 🛖 Backcountry Huts

- The Alpine Club of Canada operates a network of [backcountry huts](https://alpineclubofcanada.ca/huts/) throughout the Rockies and beyond. They use a rolling-window system: reservations open 6 months in advance for ACC members, and 3 months in advance for non-members.
